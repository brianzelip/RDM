Introduction

This report is part of the effort of HSHSL's Data Management Task Force, Spring 2016. The purpose of the Task Force is to design and implement a new library service for research data management (RDM).

This data provides a baseline look at the landscape of RDM support that academic health science libraries are either providing or referring patrons to within their broader institution.

The theoretical framework that informs our understanding of RDM support comes from a journal article[1] and subsequent webinar[2] that identify three support levels:

  1. Education: teaching patrons about RDM
  2. Consultation: working with patrons on actual projects
  3. Infrastructure: institutionally-endorsed platforms and policies for RDM
